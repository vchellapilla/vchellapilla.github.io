Hi! My name is Varshini Chellapilla. I am a journalist, writer and lifelong idealist.

I am a recent graduate with a B.A. in Print & Digital Journalism with a minor in English from the Pennsylvania State University.

I have been writing stories since I was six-years-old, with a tale about a cat stealing a mango being my earliest masterpiece.

As a journalist, I am interested in the bigger picture and the details that pull it together. I like finding answers for people that will help them make important decisions about their lives and societies.

I have written for news outlets like the Centre Daily Times, The Daily Collegian and PublicSource. My stories have ranged from pieces about the increasing air pollution in Pittsburgh and the science behind rewriting DNA at a researcher's lab, to major projects like the state of homeless college students at Penn State and the Amish way of life for disabled members of their community.
